Operations & Process Improvement

Master the fundamentals of operational analysis, process mapping, efficiency optimization, and delivering measurable improvements for RC2 clients.

⏱ ~45 min📋 12-item SOP checklist🎯 5 quiz questions
📊
What Is Operations Consulting?

Process Mapping

Visual documentation of how work flows through an organization — identifying inputs, outputs, decision points, and handoffs to spot inefficiencies.

Value-Added vs. Non-Value-Added

VA activities directly contribute to the customer's product/service. NVA activities (waste) should be minimized or eliminated to improve efficiency.

Cycle Time vs. Lead Time

Cycle time = time to complete one unit. Lead time = total time from order to delivery. Reducing the gap between them is the core goal.

Bottleneck Analysis

The constraint that limits overall throughput. Goldratt's Theory of Constraints: identify, exploit, subordinate, elevate the bottleneck.

Standard Operating Procedures

Step-by-step documented instructions that ensure consistent, quality execution of tasks regardless of who performs them.

Kaizen / Continuous Improvement

Japanese philosophy of small, incremental improvements. PDCA cycle: Plan → Do → Check → Act. Everyone in the organization contributes.

🔧
RC2 Operations Methodology

RC2 uses a 5-Phase Assessment Framework for every client engagement:


1
Discovery & BaselineMap current-state processes, interview stakeholders, gather KPI data. Document what IS happening, not what should be.
2
Gap AnalysisCompare current state to best practices, regulatory requirements, and industry benchmarks. Quantify the cost of each gap.
3
Recommendations & PrioritizationRank improvements by impact vs. effort. Build a phased roadmap with quick wins (30 days), medium-term (90 days), and strategic (12 months).
4
Implementation SupportBuild SOPs, train teams, install tracking systems. RC2 stays hands-on through execution, not just recommendations.
5
Measurement & SustainmentSet KPIs, monthly reviews, course-correct. Document lessons learned and update SOPs. Build the client's internal capability.

Real Client Scenarios
📍 Scenario 1: Manufacturing Client — Production Delays
A garment manufacturer in Honduras is consistently missing shipment deadlines. The production manager says the cutting department is to blame, but the cutting manager says they're always waiting on fabric. The owner is frustrated and has lost two buyers in the past year. What do you do first?
✅ RC2 Approach: Do NOT take sides yet. Conduct a time-study observation — physically watch the fabric-to-cut-to-sew flow for one full production day and document timestamps at each handoff. Nine times out of ten, the real bottleneck is a scheduling or communication breakdown between departments, not any one department's performance. You'll likely find fabric prep isn't synchronized with the cutting schedule. Build a production schedule board and daily standup protocol. Draft a simple internal SOP for fabric release timing. Quick win in 30 days.
📍 Scenario 2: Distribution Company — High Error Rate
A regional distribution company has a 12% order error rate (wrong items shipped, wrong quantities). They have 8 warehouse staff and no formal picking SOPs. They're losing $15K/month in returns and re-ships. What does your engagement plan look like?
✅ RC2 Approach: Map the entire pick-pack-ship process with the staff who actually do the work — they know exactly where errors happen. You'll find 80% of errors come from 2–3 root causes (usually unclear bin labeling, no verification step, or rushed end-of-shift orders). Implement: (1) barcode verification at pick, (2) visual bin labeling system, (3) a 2-minute pre-ship verification checklist, (4) error log to track by person/time/SKU. ROI is immediate and measurable — this is a great case study for RC2 proposals.
📍 Scenario 3: Client Resists Your Recommendations
You've completed a full assessment for a family-owned business. The owner agrees with your findings but says "we've always done it this way" and pushes back on implementing new SOPs. His long-tenured employees are resistant. How do you proceed?
✅ RC2 Approach: Never force change — anchor it to their pain point and their money. Reframe: "I understand — let's just try the new process for one department, for 30 days. We'll track the numbers together. If it doesn't improve error rates, we go back." Involve the long-tenured employees in building the SOP — they become co-authors, not resisters. Change management IS operations consulting. Document everything so the owner can see results in black and white.

WRAP Compliance & Audit Preparation

Understand WRAP's 12 Principles inside and out, prepare factories for certification, manage CAPs, and position RC2 as the go-to WRAP compliance partner.

⏱ ~60 min📋 14-item SOP checklist🎯 5 quiz questions
🌐
What Is WRAP?

WRAP (Worldwide Responsible Accredited Production) is an independent, objective, non-profit team of global social compliance experts dedicated to the certification of lawful, humane, and ethical manufacturing. Headquartered in Arlington, VA, WRAP is recognized by major global retailers and brands as one of the leading social compliance certifications for apparel, footwear, and sewn products factories.

WRAP Platinum

Highest level — 3-year certification with 3 full audits. Demonstrates sustained, institutional compliance culture. Major retailers specifically seek Platinum suppliers.

WRAP Gold

2-year certification with 2 audits. Most common starting target for first-time applicants. Requires passing all 12 principles at a high level.

WRAP Silver

1-year certification with 1 audit. Entry-level path. Excellent goal for factories beginning their compliance journey — can upgrade to Gold/Platinum.

CAP (Corrective Action Plan)

Required document when an audit finding shows non-compliance. Must include root cause analysis, corrective action, responsible party, and completion date.

PASA (Pre-Audit Self-Assessment)

Factory completes this before the formal audit. RC2 uses the PASA Scorecard to identify gaps before the auditor arrives — critical prep step.

Monitoring Audit

Unannounced follow-up visits by WRAP-accredited monitors between certification cycles. Factories must remain in compliance at all times — not just during audits.

📋
The 12 WRAP Principles — Quick Reference
P1 — Laws & Workplace Regulations: Comply with all local laws and regulations
P2 — Prohibition of Forced Labor: No involuntary or coerced labor of any kind
P3 — Prohibition of Child Labor: No workers under legal age; age verification mandatory
P4 — Prohibition of Harassment: No harassment or abuse — physical, sexual, verbal, psychological
P5 — Compensation & Benefits: Pay legally required wages & benefits on time
P6 — Hours of Work: Legal limits on hours, mandatory rest periods and days off
P7 — Prohibition of Discrimination: Fair treatment regardless of race, gender, religion, etc.
P8 — Health, Safety & Environment: Safe working conditions, fire safety, chemical handling
P9 — Freedom of Association: Right to organize and bargain collectively per local law
P10 — Environment: Comply with environmental laws; responsible waste/chemical disposal
P11 — Customs Compliance: Accurate manifests, anti-smuggling, C-TPAT alignment if applicable
P12 — Drug-Free Workplace: Written policy, testing protocols, employee awareness programs

Real Client Scenarios
📍 Scenario 1: Payroll Records Are Incomplete
During PASA prep, you discover the factory has paper payroll records from 2023 but switched to a digital system in 2024 — and the digital records from Q1 2024 are missing. The audit is in 6 weeks. What do you do?
✅ RC2 Approach: Immediately escalate to the factory owner — this is a critical gap. Work with HR and accounting to reconstruct Q1 2024 records from bank statements, tax filings, and any partial records. Document the reconstruction process in writing. Draft a CAP showing the gap, the reconstruction effort, and the new records retention policy being implemented. Auditors understand transitions happen — what they need to see is a credible, documented response, not perfection.
📍 Scenario 2: Worker Interview Coaching Requests
The factory owner asks you to "coach" workers on what to say when the WRAP auditor interviews them privately. He says other factories do this. How do you respond?
✅ RC2 Approach: This is a bright-line issue. RC2 NEVER coaches workers to provide false or misleading information to auditors. This would constitute fraud and could result in immediate certification revocation and blacklisting. Explain this clearly and professionally to the owner: "RC2's value is in fixing actual problems, not hiding them. If workers are saying something negative, we need to hear it and address it — that's what will get you certified and keep you certified." This is also a good moment to assess whether this is a client RC2 wants to work with long-term.

Supply Chain & Logistics Management

Navigate vendor management, nearshoring strategy, Incoterms, freight modes, and supply chain risk mitigation for RC2 clients in manufacturing and distribution.

⏱ ~45 min📋 10-item SOP checklist🎯 5 quiz questions
🚢
Core Supply Chain Concepts

Incoterms 2020

International Commercial Terms defining who is responsible for shipping, insurance, and tariffs. Key terms: FOB (Factory/Port), CIF, DDP, EXW. Every RC2 client dealing in imports/exports must understand these.

FOB (Free on Board)

Seller is responsible until goods are loaded onto the vessel. After that, buyer assumes risk and cost. Most common term for apparel manufacturing from Asia/Central America.

DDP (Delivered Duty Paid)

Seller handles everything including customs clearance and import duties at the destination. Most buyer-friendly. Seller assumes maximum risk and cost.

Nearshoring

Moving manufacturing/sourcing from distant countries (Asia) to geographically closer regions (Central America, Mexico, Caribbean). Benefits: shorter lead times, lower freight costs, USMCA/CAFTA duty advantages.

Vendor Due Diligence

Systematic evaluation of suppliers across financial stability, compliance, capacity, and quality. RC2's Vendor Due Diligence Checklist covers 6 dimensions with weighted scoring.

Tariff Classification (HTS)

Every imported product has an HTS code determining the duty rate. Correct classification is legally required — misclassification can result in penalties, back-duties, and import delays.

🌎
Americas Trade Landscape — RC2 Focus Region
USMCA — US-Mexico-Canada Agreement. Replaced NAFTA in 2020. Zero tariffs on qualifying goods with sufficient regional content. Critical for RC2 clients in Mexico.
CAFTA-DR — US-Central America-Dominican Republic FTA. Covers Guatemala, Honduras, El Salvador, Nicaragua, Costa Rica, DR. Most garment/textile factories operate under this.
Haiti HOPE/HELP — Special trade preferences for Haiti. Allows duty-free apparel exports to the US under specific quota. RC2 has deep expertise in this program.
Section 301/232 — US tariff actions against China and others. Driving nearshoring trend. Clients sourcing from China face 25–145% additional duties depending on product category.
✅

Real Client Scenarios
📍 Scenario 1: Client Asks About China Tariff Impact
A US-based apparel brand sources 85% of its product from China. With tariffs up to 145% on Chinese goods in 2025, their landed costs have nearly doubled. They ask RC2 to help them find a solution. What's your strategy?
✅ RC2 Approach: Run the RC2 Tariff Impact Analysis Tool immediately to quantify exact duty exposure by product category. Then build a nearshoring analysis comparing Honduras, Guatemala, or DR (CAFTA-DR, 0% duty) vs. current China sourcing. Factor in: lead time increase, minimum order changes, quality investment needed, and transition timeline. Most clients can achieve 30–50% cost savings on duties alone by nearshoring — often more than offsetting higher per-unit production costs. Build a 12-month transition plan with a pilot program on 1–2 SKUs first.
📍 Scenario 2: Vendor Fails Due Diligence Midway Through Contract
You discover a key vendor for a client has severe financial instability — 90-day past due on multiple supplier invoices — during a mid-contract due diligence review. There are 3 shipments in transit. What do you do?
✅ RC2 Approach: Immediate client notification — this is a fiduciary responsibility. Advise the client to: (1) confirm current shipments are insured and track closely, (2) pause any new purchase orders pending vendor review, (3) activate backup supplier protocol immediately, (4) legal review of contract terms regarding vendor default. Do NOT panic the client — present this as a risk you caught early through proactive diligence. This is exactly the value RC2 provides.

Government Contracting

Master SAM.gov registration, SBA certifications (8(a), WOSB, HUBZone, SDVOSB), FAR/DFARS compliance, and set-aside bidding strategy for federal contracts.

⏱ ~50 min📋 10-item SOP checklist🎯 5 quiz questions
🏛️
Government Contracting Essentials

SAM.gov

System for Award Management — every business MUST register here before bidding on federal contracts. Free to register, must renew annually. UEI number issued upon registration.

NAICS Codes

North American Industry Classification System. Businesses select primary and secondary codes. SBA size standards (employee count or revenue) are determined per NAICS code.

8(a) Program

SBA program for socially & economically disadvantaged business owners. 9-year program with access to set-aside contracts. Requires direct ownership and control by a disadvantaged individual.

WOSB / EDWOSB

Women-Owned Small Business certification. EDWOSB = Economically Disadvantaged WOSB. Provides access to set-aside contracts in industries where women are underrepresented.

FAR (Federal Acquisition Regulation)

The primary rulebook governing all federal procurement. Every government contractor must comply with relevant FAR clauses included in their contract.

Set-Aside Contracts

Federal contracts reserved exclusively for small businesses or specific certification categories. In FY2024, the federal government spent $778B — 23% set-aside for small business. Massive opportunity.

Scaling Fundamentals

The Founder Trap

When all decisions flow through one person, growth is capped at that person's capacity. RC2's job is to build systems and people that free the founder from daily operations.

Organizational Design

Intentionally structuring roles, responsibilities, and reporting lines to support growth. RACI matrix documents who is Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed for each function.

Scalable Systems

ERP, CRM, HRMS, WMS — technology that handles increasing volume without linear headcount increases. RC2 helps clients select and implement right-sized systems.

SOPs as Infrastructure

Written, tested standard operating procedures are the foundation of scalability. They enable training, delegation, consistency, and quality control as headcount grows.

Capacity Planning

Projecting resource needs (people, equipment, space) against projected demand. Prevents both over-investment and the more common problem — being unable to fulfill demand due to infrastructure gaps.

KPI-Driven Management

Replacing gut-feel with data. 6–10 key metrics that tell the story of business health weekly. Leaders who manage by KPIs scale faster and make better decisions under pressure.

KPI Framework & Performance Management

Build meaningful KPI systems that drive decision-making, align teams with strategy, and give clients clear visibility into operational and financial health.

⏱ ~35 min📋 8-item checklist🎯 4 quiz questions
📊
KPI Fundamentals

KPI vs. Metric

Not all metrics are KPIs. A KPI is a metric that directly indicates progress toward a strategic objective. Choose 6–10 true KPIs, not 40 random metrics.

SMART Targets

Every KPI needs a SMART target: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound. "Improve quality" is NOT a KPI. "Reduce defect rate below 2% by Q3" IS.

Leading vs. Lagging Indicators

Lagging: measures outcomes (revenue, profit). Leading: measures drivers of future outcomes (sales pipeline, units produced per day). Best KPI systems use both.

RC2's 6 KPI Domains

Financial, Operational Efficiency, Quality & Compliance, Customer/Client, Team & Workforce, and Strategic Growth. Cover all 6 domains for a complete picture.

Reporting Cadence

Daily operational KPIs (production, safety). Weekly management KPIs (sales, throughput). Monthly executive KPIs (financial, compliance). Match cadence to decision-making need.

KPI Ownership

Every KPI must have ONE named owner responsible for results and reporting. Anonymous KPIs are ignored. Ownership drives accountability.

✅
KPI Implementation Checklist

Documentation, SOPs & Training Programs

Build, organize, and deploy comprehensive SOP libraries and employee training programs that create consistency, reduce errors, and enable rapid onboarding.

⏱ ~40 min📋 10-item checklist🎯 4 quiz questions
📄
SOP Best Practices

Anatomy of a Good SOP

Title, purpose, scope, responsibility, frequency, step-by-step procedure, expected outcome, and revision history. Missing any of these = incomplete SOP.

Reading Level Rule

Write SOPs at a 6th–8th grade reading level. If a new employee can't follow it on day 1, rewrite it. Complexity in SOPs signals unclear thinking, not sophistication.

Visual SOPs

Flowcharts, photos, and decision trees dramatically improve compliance. For manufacturing floor SOPs, visual aids are non-negotiable — never text-only.

Version Control

Every SOP needs a version number, effective date, and approval signature. Old versions must be physically removed when updated. Confusion between versions is a compliance risk.

Training vs. SOPs

SOPs tell workers WHAT to do. Training programs explain WHY and build the skill to DO it. Both are required. SOPs without training = shelfware. Training without SOPs = no consistency.

Competency Verification

Training must be verifiable. Observation-based sign-off, written tests, or practical demonstrations prove workers can do the task — not just that they attended training.

Tariffs, Trade & Importing/Exporting

Navigate the 2025–2026 tariff landscape, Section 301/232 actions, IEEPA emergency authorities, customs entry requirements, and practical strategies for managing tariff exposure.

⏱ ~45 min📋 10-item checklist🎯 5 quiz questions
🌐
2025–2026 Tariff Landscape

Section 301 Tariffs (China)

Imposed under the Trade Act of 1974 for unfair trade practices. China tariffs reached 145% on many categories in 2025. Massive driver of sourcing shift to Americas.

Section 232 Tariffs (Steel/Aluminum)

National security tariffs. 25% on steel imports, 10% on aluminum from most countries. Directly impacts manufacturing clients using metal inputs.

IEEPA Emergency Authority

International Emergency Economic Powers Act — broad presidential authority to impose tariffs in national emergencies. Used in 2025 for expanded tariff actions. Subject to legal challenge.

First Sale Valuation

Customs valuation method using the first sale price in a chain of transactions (factory → importer) rather than the final sale price. Can significantly reduce dutiable value.

Bonded Warehouse / FTZ

Foreign Trade Zone: goods can be stored, manipulated, or manufactured without paying duties until they enter US commerce. Powerful tool for managing tariff cash flow.

Customs Bond

Required for all importers of record. Continuous bond covers all imports for the year; single-entry bond for one shipment. Must be sufficient to cover potential duty liability.

⚠️
2025–2026 Critical Tariff Timeline
Jan 2025
Trump administration reinstated and expanded Section 301 tariffs on Chinese goods. Additional 10% baseline tariff announced on all imports via IEEPA.
Apr 2025
China tariffs escalated to 145% on many categories. "Liberation Day" tariff package announced — 10–50% tariffs on 60+ countries. Most paused 90 days.
May 2025
Federal courts began challenging IEEPA tariff authority. USMCA countries largely preserved at 0% for qualifying goods — major nearshoring incentive confirmed.
Ongoing
Americas nearshoring acceleration — CAFTA-DR and USMCA manufacturers seeing surge in orders as US brands diversify away from China. RC2 opportunity zone.
