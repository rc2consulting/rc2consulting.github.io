👔 What is a Fractional Director of Operations?

A Fractional DOO provides senior-level operational leadership to businesses that need an experienced executive but cannot justify a full-time hire. As an RC2 Fractional DOO, you are embedded into the client's organization on a part-time basis, responsible for designing systems, building accountability, and driving measurable operational performance.

Not a consultant — an operatorPart-time, full impactTypically $1M–$15M revenue clients3–12 month engagements
🎯
Your Core Mission
Design and implement operational systems that allow the business to scale without the owner being in every decision. You create order from chaos and replace heroics with systems.
🔑
Key Responsibilities
Weekly leadership meetings, KPI tracking and reporting, SOP development and implementation, team accountability systems, vendor management, process optimization, and monthly executive reporting to ownership.
📊
What You Measure
Revenue and margin trends, on-time delivery, defect rates, team productivity, SOP adoption, process cycle times, employee satisfaction, and cost per unit. If it cannot be measured, it cannot be managed.
🚫
What You Do NOT Do
You do not do the work for the team — you build the system so the team does the work correctly. You do not manage day-to-day tasks. You lead, coach, design, and measure. You are a builder, not a doer.
💬
How You Communicate
Weekly: team meeting facilitation and status updates. Monthly: written executive report to owner. Continuously: document everything in writing. No verbal-only agreements. No undocumented commitments.
⚠️
Common Pitfalls to Avoid
Scope creep (doing non-DOO work), skipping documentation, making verbal promises without SOPs, taking over instead of coaching, failing to establish KPIs in Month 1, and not protecting your time on non-deliverables.
💰
How RC2 Bills This Service
Monthly retainer: $4,500–$10,000/month depending on hours and engagement complexity. One-time onboarding fee ($1,500–$2,500) for assessment and setup. Bill in advance, 30-day cancellation clause minimum.
✅
Definition of Success
The business runs smoothly without the owner in every decision. KPIs improve measurably each month. The team follows documented SOPs consistently. The owner has visibility into operations through dashboards and reports — not through firefighting.

The RC2 DOO Framework
Month 1: Assess & Listen. Month 2: Design & Build. Month 3+: Implement, Train & Optimize. Every engagement follows this pattern. Never skip the assessment phase — it is where you earn your credibility.

🎯 The Non-Negotiable Rules of the Weekly Rhythm
Rule 1: Meetings Start on Time
Leadership meetings always start at the scheduled time — even if the owner is not present. This sets the tone that time is respected and accountability is real.
Rule 2: Action Items Are Always Documented
Every action item from every meeting gets written down: what, who, by when. No verbal-only assignments. Use a shared document or project tracker.
Rule 3: No Meeting Without an Agenda
Send the agenda at least 24 hours before every leadership meeting. Attendees must come prepared. Meetings without agendas are conversations — not leadership.
Rule 4: KPIs Are Reviewed Weekly
Every leadership meeting opens with a 10-minute KPI review. No exceptions. If a number is red, the responsible party must have an explanation and a corrective action ready.
📊 Build the Client's KPI Framework
A KPI framework is the client's operational scoreboard. Your job is to define the right metrics in Month 1, establish baselines, and report on them every week and every month. Start with 8–12 KPIs maximum. Do not overwhelm the client.

RC2 Rule: Never accept "we don't track that" as a final answer. Your job is to build the tracking system. Start with manual tracking if needed. Automate later. The most important thing is that the number gets measured, even imperfectly, starting this week.

📐 RC2 Standard KPI Categories for a Fractional DOO Engagement
💰
Financial KPIs
Revenue, gross margin, cost per unit, accounts receivable days, monthly burn rate, and revenue per employee. These connect operations to the bottom line.
⚙️
Operations KPIs
On-time delivery rate, production efficiency, defect/rework rate, inventory turnover, order cycle time, and capacity utilization.
👥
People KPIs
Employee turnover rate, training completion, satisfaction score, open positions aging, absenteeism rate, and productivity per employee.
🤝
Customer KPIs
Customer satisfaction score, complaint resolution time, return/refund rate, repeat purchase rate, and net promoter score (NPS).
👥 Accountability Matrix (RACI Model)
The RACI matrix defines who is Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed for each key business function. Build this with the client in Month 1. It eliminates confusion, duplicate effort, and the "I didn't know that was my job" problem.

R = Responsible (does the work)  |  A = Accountable (owns the outcome — only ONE person)  |  C = Consulted (provides input)  |  I = Informed (notified of outcomes)
