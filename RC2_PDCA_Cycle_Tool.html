What Is the PDCA Cycle?

The Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA) cycle — also known as the Deming Cycle or Shewhart Cycle — is the foundational continuous improvement methodology used by world-class organizations across manufacturing, services, government contracting, and compliance-regulated industries.

Originated by Walter Shewhart in the 1930s and popularized by W. Edwards Deming in the 1950s, PDCA is embedded in ISO 9001:2015, WRAP compliance standards, and Lean/Six Sigma frameworks. It is not a one-time project — it is a permanent operating rhythm for organizations committed to measurable, sustained improvement.

P PLAN

Identify the problem, analyze root causes, set measurable objectives, and design the improvement approach.

  • Define the problem with data
  • Analyze root causes (5-Why, Fishbone)
  • Set SMART objectives and targets
  • Design the action plan with owners and deadlines

D DO

Execute the plan on a small scale first. Implement the changes, collect data, and document what happens.

  • Pilot the improvement (controlled scope)
  • Train affected personnel
  • Execute the action plan step by step
  • Collect data during implementation

C CHECK

Measure the results against your objectives. Did the change produce the expected improvement? What deviated?

  • Compare results to targets
  • Analyze variance — what worked, what didn’t
  • Identify unintended consequences
  • Validate data integrity

A ACT

If the improvement worked, standardize it. If not, adjust and cycle again. Document lessons learned.

  • Standardize successful changes into SOPs
  • Train the broader organization
  • Identify the next improvement target
  • Start the next PDCA cycle
Why PDCA Matters for Your Organization

Compliance & Audit Readiness

PDCA is the core improvement methodology required by ISO 9001:2015 (Section 10.3) and embedded in WRAP audit standards. Demonstrating a functioning PDCA cycle is audit evidence of a mature quality management system.

Government Contracting

Federal agencies evaluate contractors on continuous improvement capability. A documented PDCA process demonstrates the operational maturity that differentiates winning proposals from competitors.

Operational Excellence

Organizations using structured PDCA cycles resolve operational problems 3.2x faster than those using ad hoc approaches. Source: Deloitte, “Performance Management Reinvented,” 2024.

Is PDCA Proprietary?

No. PDCA is a public-domain methodology. It is not patented, trademarked, or proprietary. Any organization can freely adopt, teach, and implement PDCA without licensing fees or restrictions.
